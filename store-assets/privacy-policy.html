TabFlow — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

What TabFlow Does

TabFlow is a browser tab manager that organizes your tabs into workspaces. It reads your open tab URLs and titles so it can display and manage them within the extension.

Data Stored Locally

All workspace and tab data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage APIs (chrome.storage.local). This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly enable cloud sync.

Cloud Sync (Optional)

If you choose to enable cloud sync, your workspace data is encrypted on your device using AES-GCM encryption before it is sent to your own self-hosted Supabase database. The extension developer has no access to your Supabase project or your data. Your encryption passphrase never leaves your device.

Cloud sync is entirely optional. TabFlow works fully offline with local-only storage.

Data Collected

Web history (tab URLs and titles): Used solely to display and organize your tabs within the extension. This data is stored locally and, if cloud sync is enabled, encrypted and synced to your own database.

No other data is collected. TabFlow does not collect analytics, telemetry, personal information, browsing behavior, or any data beyond what is needed to manage your tabs.

Third-Party Services

TabFlow does not send data to any third-party services. If you enable cloud sync, data is sent only to the Supabase project URL that you configure yourself.

No Ads, No Tracking

TabFlow contains no advertisements, no analytics scripts, and no tracking of any kind.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, reach out to the developer directly.
